 Research on Tezos Ecosystem Projects

Course: Tezos Developer Course

Assignment Objective: The objective of this assignment is to familiarize students with the Tezos ecosystem and its diverse range of projects and applications. By researching and presenting on a Tezos-based project, students will gain insights into the practical applications of Tezos technology and its impact on the blockchain space.

Instructions:

Assignment Task:

Project Selection (10 points):

Instruct students to select a Tezos-based project or application from the Tezos ecosystem. Encourage them to explore a variety of projects, such as decentralized applications (DApps), decentralized finance (DeFi) platforms, or governance solutions.
Project Overview (30 points):

Ask students to provide a comprehensive overview of the selected project. Their overview should include:
Project name and description.
Project goals and objectives.
Use cases or real-world problems the project aims to solve.
Key features or innovations offered by the project.
Tezos Utilization (30 points):

Instruct students to delve into how the chosen project utilizes Tezos technology. They should explain:
How Tezos smart contracts are used within the project.
The role of Tezos tokens (XTZ) within the project's ecosystem.
Any unique features or advantages of using Tezos for the project.
Impact Assessment (20 points):

Encourage students to discuss the potential impact of the project on the broader blockchain space. They should consider factors like:
How the project addresses current challenges or inefficiencies in blockchain technology.
Its potential to drive innovation or adoption in the blockchain industry.
Any partnerships or collaborations that enhance the project's influence.
Presentation (10 points):

Ask students to present their research findings to the class. Presentations can be in the form of slides, reports, or spoken presentations, depending on the class format.
Submission Guidelines:

Students should submit their project research reports or presentation materials to the instructor or the designated platform provided for the assignment.
Grading Rubric:

Project Selection (10 points)
Project Overview (30 points)
Tezos Utilization (30 points)
Impact Assessment (20 points)
Presentation (10 points)
