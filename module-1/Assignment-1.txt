Blockchain Fundamentals Research

Course: Tezos Developer Course

Assignment Objective: The objective of this assignment is to provide students with a solid foundation in blockchain technology by researching and understanding its fundamental concepts, functionality, and real-world applications.

Instructions:

Assignment Task:

Blockchain Fundamentals Research

Research (40 points):

Instruct students to conduct in-depth research on blockchain technology. They should explore and understand the core concepts, principles, and components of blockchain.
Students should research and provide explanations for key topics, including but not limited to:
What is blockchain technology, and how does it differ from traditional databases?
How does blockchain achieve decentralization and consensus?
What is a distributed ledger, and how does it work in a blockchain context?
Explain the concept of cryptographic hashing and digital signatures in blockchain.
Explore the role of miners, validators, and nodes in a blockchain network.
Discuss the importance of immutability and transparency in blockchain.
Provide insights into blockchain security and potential vulnerabilities.
Use Cases and Examples (30 points):

Instruct students to research and include real-world use cases and examples of blockchain applications. They should showcase how blockchain technology has been adopted in various industries, such as finance, supply chain, healthcare, and more.
Encourage students to provide case studies or examples of specific blockchain projects, highlighting the problems they solve and the benefits they offer.
Students should demonstrate a clear understanding of how blockchain is reshaping industries and solving real-world challenges.
Report Writing (20 points):

Ask students to compile their research findings into a well-structured report. The report should be well-organized, with sections covering each of the research topics mentioned above.
Emphasize the importance of clear and concise explanations, using appropriate terminology and examples to illustrate concepts.
Students should properly cite their sources and provide references for the information used in their report.
Presentation (10 points):

Have students present their research findings to the class or submit a recorded presentation summarizing the key points of their report.
Encourage students to use visuals, diagrams, or slides to enhance their presentations and make the content more engaging.
Submission Guidelines:

Students should submit their research report and presentation (if applicable) to the instructor or the designated platform provided for the assignment.
Grading Rubric:

Research (40 points)
Use Cases and Examples (30 points)
Report Writing (20 points)
Presentation (10 points)
